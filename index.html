What is a token, blockchain and NFT?

A token is a unit of account in a blockchain network that can be used to create digital values. A blockchain is a decentralized network that operates on a sequential chain of blocks that contain information about token transactions. Blockchain entries are not subject to change and are public, so this avoids data manipulation and ensures their security

Tokens in such networks are of two types: interchangeable (fungible tokens) and non-interchangeable (non-fungible token). Here's a simple example: the well-known cryptocurrency bitcoin is an interchangeable token, as any bitcoin has the same value as another and can be exchanged with other cryptocurrencies. "Non-interchangeable" in this context means that it is unique, both in terms of the object it describes and its value. That is, a collector's image, which is represented by a token and is the only one of its kind, cannot be replaced by another similar one, because another one in nature no longer exist. In other words, NFT itself is a type of digital asset created on a blockchain, which allows you to obtain ownership of a product that exists exclusively on the network, such as images, animations or videos, while certifying its originality.

How does it work?

From a technological point of view, an NFT asset is a digital certificate attached to any "digital product", such as images, video, audio, and so on. This certificate (token) will contain all the information about the product. The token is the exclusive right to the product, so owning it, buying, selling or exchanging, all these transactions are carried out with the product itself. We can always find out who created such a product or who now owns it, because the tokens are stored in an open blockchain and because of this information about the product, the history of its operations will be available and reliable always.So, this is an ideal opportunity to claim your rights to any unique object.

Digital art is the art of the new age!

Let's return to the issue of digital art. It turned out to be the most appropriate and demonstrative for the masses in terms of the possibility of using NFT and its promotion.There are many challenges for digital artists today. One is that digital art objects can be easily copied and done as many times as you like. Yes, many will say that you can just save a picture and such saved copies can be many that do not differ from the original. But there is one nuance, or rather the opportunity provided by NFT - is the right to own the original version of the work. It is like having an original painting on display at the Louvre, and other reproductions and copies outside it will only promote and increase the value of this work, as it will become more recognizable. Therefore, NFT allows you to capture your intellectual property rights, which are confirmed in the blockchain.According to experts, the use of NFT can be a new tool in the field of intellectual property management, create new opportunities for the market and its participants, making it more convenient, because token transactions are cheap, simple and faster than transactions with real objects to which they tied. NFT tools also open up new possibilities for authors of works of art. For example, you can tokenize and sell your work on special sites such as OpenSea, Rarible, Nifty Gateway. The peculiarity is that on some of them you will receive interest, each time your work is resold. And, as you know, if an art object becomes popular, its value increases, and you, as the author, will earn even more. And it does not depend on the desire or unwillingness to pay you, because the commission (royalty) is spelled out in a digital smart contract and will be charged to you automatically.

A new tool for intellectual property

The huge popularity and growth forecasts of this segment of blockchain technology (which is already becoming a completely autonomous market) make us think about its relationship with intellectual property rights and copyright in particular. NFT tokens have proven that anything can be digitized, and this is a kind of new stage for intellectual property in the digital world. Intellectual property is usually unique, and patents and certificates for these objects certify the exclusive intellectual property right to it. Security documents (patents, certificates) contain information about the object itself, the intellectual property right and the time of registration and validity of this right. This is very similar to what the NFT token itself contains, right?

Prospects for NFT tokenization

In themselves, the terms of smart contracts that apply to the tokenization of digital assets may be quite common in the intellectual property law of most jurisdictions (regarding the transfer of rights, payment of remuneration, authorization of certain uses and control over such use). Therefore, ensuring compliance with the requirements and standards for the distribution of works as copyrighted works with national law through NFT is not a critical issue. But it still needs a solution. This is especially true for defining the legal nature of the token itself as a digital asset. Given that "everything can be tokenized", it is sometimes problematic to define it as an intangible / digital asset, IP property rights or securities. Can they only be converted into currency, or used as a stand-alone entity (for investment, lending, or inclusion in the authorized capital of enterprises)? There are no unambiguous answers to these questions today. In Ukraine, tokenization of assets is not popular and sufficiently developed, as there are features of the legal framework. The adoption of the Verkhovna Rada of Ukraine in the first reading of the draft law № 3637 “On Virtual Assets” can be considered a positive step in this direction. The draft law provides for changes in the legal relations arising in connection with the circulation of virtual assets in Ukraine, defines the rights and obligations of market participants, the principles of state policy in the field of virtual assets.
